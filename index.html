9 Hidden Secrets Of Short-Term Rental Property Management

Sunny BNB

In the realm of property investment, entrusting your asset to a short-term rental (STR) manager for platforms like Airbnb, Booking.com, and Stayz is a significant step. While financial returns and effective communication often dominate an owner's criteria for evaluating a property manager's performance, there's a broader spectrum of quality indicators that might be flying under your radar.

Let's delve into the less obvious yet equally crucial aspects that signify top-tier management, leading up to the most surprising and potentially costly secret kept until the end.

Broad Platform Exposure

  • Is your property showcased across all major Online Travel Agencies (OTAs) such as Airbnb, Booking.com, and Stayz? Expanding your property's online presence maximizes visibility and booking potential.

Professional Presentation

  • The impact of professional photography cannot be overstated. A property manager should ensure your listing features high-quality images that not only highlight your property's best attributes but also the allure of the surrounding area.Photos taken by the manager on their iphone, or iPad are not acceptable. Likewise professional photos that are artistic rather than STR suitable, where they show vases, chairs, side boards, mirrors but leave out all photos of bedrooms, kitchen etc are not suitable.

Transparent Operations

  • Access to a live, real-time owner's portal, including a bookings calendar and detailed breakdowns, is essential. This transparency allows you to monitor all bookings and manage your property more effectively.

Understanding Management Fees

  • Clarifying management fees is critical. We see that a fair structure involves the guests covering cleaning and consumables, with the property owners paying a clear-cut management fee on accommodation revenue. Beware of seemingly low fees that exclude essential services or add unexpected charges. Especially be wary of onsite management where fees can be between 40-52%. Onsite most often means much expensive for the property owner.

Freedom To Choose Management

  • Some property oweners feel bound to insite management due to misleading advice. However, laws such as the Trade Practices Act support your right to select an external manager, often resulting in substantial savings and improved service.

Booking Flexibility

  • Restrictions on minimum nights can significantly impact your earnings, especially since a large fraction of bookings are for short stays. Managers might impose longer minimums to reduce cleaning frequency, not in your financial interest.

No Hidden Fees

  • A transparent fee structure should cover most operational costs, with the guest bearing the expense of cleaning, linen, and consumables. Any additional charges, particularly for routine maintenance, should be scrutinized to ensure they're justified and don't erode your profits.

Owner Usage Rights

  • Some managers may impose unreasonable restrictions on how often you can use your property, under the guise of maximizing rental income. A balanced approach allows owners reasonable access without significantly hampering the property's earning potential.

Booking Calendar Integrity

  • The most shocking secret involves the manipulation of booking calendars by some onsite managers. They might block your property from new bookings immediately following a guest's departure to spread cleaning tasks throughout the week, potentially costing you thousands in lost income. Ensuring your property is available for new guests as soon as it's clean is critical for maximizing revenue.

In navigating the complexities of STR management, understanding these hidden aspects can empower property owners to make informed decisions, ensuring their investment is both profitable and well-maintained. The key is to partner with a property manager who values transparency, efficiency, and owner empowerment, transforming potential challenges into opportunities for success.
